    Comparison with other software

    Jess Hagman Social Sciences Research Librarian of the University of Illinois has written a comparative article of free QDA software including QualCoder for the Anthropology and Sociology Section for the Association of College Research Libraries. ANSS Currents, Vol 36, No 2, Fall 2021.

    The PDF is located here: https://acrl.ala.org/anss/

    It’s nice to see QualCoder listed as a good free alternative to Nvivo. How to Download Nvivo for Students (Windows/Mac)

    Thank you to SoftCualitativo who have put together some tables describing similarities and differences between QualCoder, Atlas.ti and MaxQDA.

    Software CAQDAS y su Integración con Inteligencia Artificial

    One of the considerations in adopting and open-source free program is usability and whether it has the same essential features as popular commercial tools.

    This workshop will look at QualCoder and compare it with these commercial programs on ten dimensions (1) installation and customization; (2) supported files/data types; (3) workspace, project, file and data management; (4) cases and files classification; (5) memos, annotations and journals; (6) coding (manual, auto and AI assisted); (7) visualization; (8) analysis, querying and reporting; (9) collaboration; and (10) converting to/from other qualitative data analysis tools.

    The Workshop was conducted 12th May by Van Tran Temple University Librarian. The YouTube video is below:

    Article about AI and qualitative research published May 2025 at the University of Surrey, by Dr Kai Droege. Why AI has a "proving the obvious" problem, and what we can do about it | Computer-Assisted Qualitative Data Analysis

    This presentation by Prof. Erin Owens from the Sam Houston State University shows two free qualitative options: Taguette and QualCoder. The QualCoder version shown is the older 3.5 version but still worth viewing.

    Thank you to Alfonso Robles for creating this post in Spanish for Qualcoder 3.0.

    Alfonso Robles has made a YouTube video (in Spanish) to create a coding match matrix and GEPHI Network Visualisation using data from QualCoder and the R statistics package.

    You need to extract the data in QualCoder and export to a csv file. The data source is from the menu Reports > Database queries. Then in the list on the side: Default Queries > GET CODING TABLE
